# Advice

It is a pity that advising cannot but include advice. There is very good advice
from the [Programming Language Mentoring
Workshops](https://www.sigplan.org/Conferences/PLMW/). Many of the talks are
available online on YouTube. Here are the ones that I particularly liked:

+ [Simon Peyton Jones: How To Read a Research Paper: Seven Simple Ideas @ ICFP 2024](https://youtu.be/e1vmUI59yHY?si=yBX9WdFMOFejU8sN)
+ [Benjamin Pierce: Imagining the Reader @ POPL 2023](https://youtu.be/Q3Ye58zirjU?si=08vs0mWY41yQ8Dxk)
+ [Derek Dreyer: How to write papers and give talks that people can follow @ ICFP 2017](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZmFXMREURBI)
+ [Derek Dreyer: How To Write Papers So People Can Read Them @ ICFP 2020](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XpgJ31GKPWI)
+ [Aws Albarghouthi: Uncertainty and my scientific journey @ PLDI 2024](https://youtu.be/kYZiPZ3EwRc?si=oG4X0daDbWJmD_Ij)
